    My Second to None Military Service as a KATUSA Soldier

    KATUSA, an acronym for Korean Augmentation To the United States Army, is a branch in the Republic of Korea Army. KATUSA program established back in July, 1950 during the heavy fire of Korean War as a verbal agreement between the Korean President Lee and U.S. General MacArthur. I began my service for my country on 25 June 2012. After graduating the basic training and AIT (Advanced Individual Training), I was assigned to HHC, 1ABCT, 2ID at Camp Hovey, Korea. I continued to served in the unit until my ETS(Expiration of Term of Service) in March, 2014.
